A Look into Values

  • Phase 0: Week 7

When I was young, one of the first books my parents had me read was The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People, and between that and being a religious Jew, values have always been a strong part of my life. One value I try to live every single day by is love. I want to give and recieve love in everything from my work to my attitude to my relationships. On a scale of never to always, I generally always try to live up to my ideal. I once heard the quote, "we are what we repeatedly do, excellence, therefore, is not an act but a habit." I think you can subsitute excellence here in for any value- love, curiousity, generosity, kindness... We become what we repeatedly do.

I was recently asked advice on this very topic by a dear friend: how could she balance her loyalty and love to the organization she worked for with the struggle of having outgrown it? I responded with how I would handle the situation, exhuding that love value: respectfully and lovingly thank the organization and leave them well off, but recognize when it's important to love oneself and pursue other opportunities.

But I believe holding onto these values, in particular your strongest value, is incredibly important especially when things get hard. I hope that at DBC I can remember this (particularly when I get into fights with my computer!) Even in the case of stereotype threat, it can be used to acknowledge that I'm above being defined by any stereotype. Meditating and lighting candles usually help me take a few moments to get back in touch with my values, and when a need a bigger tune up I find it a great exercise to write/revisit my life mission statement. I think I'll write a DBC mission statement as well, steeped in my values, to check back in with.
